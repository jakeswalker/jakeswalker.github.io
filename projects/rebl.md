## Background

Prior to developing this app, I realized that I was picking up my phone out of habit, and I was opening the Instagram app subconsciously. I did this multiple times each day, and each time I open the Instagram, I am down a rabbit hole and spend about thirty minutes or sometimes even an hour. Around this time, I also came across an anti-Facebook social media service called Ello. On its website I came across a post by its founder, Paul Budnitz, which he talked about his struggle with anxiety and his decision to abandon his phone and adopt a “dumb phone” called Punkt. Inspired by his post, I decided to make an iOS app that will help people “digital detox.” My app’s concept was to break the habit of picking up the phone subconsciously to check social media. A user can set a specific time to check the social media and set it to send a notification at that time. The user would also place the app next to the social media apps on the home screen, so that if they pick up a phone subconsciously to check social media, they would open this app instead, which will only show a count down until the time the user is allowed to check social media. This app had a few thousands downloads from around the world, which shows that many people around the world struggle from social media addiction.
