Rebl: Look Around

An iOS application that I designed, programmed, and deployed using Illustrator, Objective-C, and Xcode. It is designed to help people reduce the amount of time spent on social media apps.

Background

Prior to developing this app, I realized that I was picking up my phone out of habit, and I was opening the social media apps subconsciously. I did this multiple times each day, and each time I opened the social media apps, I would spend about thirty minutes or sometimes even an hour scrolling through each app. Around this time, I also came across an anti-Facebook social media service called Ello. On its website I came across a post by its founder, Paul Budnitz, in which he talked about his struggle with anxiety and his decision to abandon his smartphone and adopt a “dumbphone” called Punkt. Inspired by his post, I decided to make an iOS app that will help people “digitally detox.”

How it works

My app’s concept was to break the habit of picking up the phone subconsciously to check the social media apps. A user can set a specific time to check social media and set it to send a notification at that time. The user would also place the app next to the social media apps on the home screen, so that if they pick up the phone subconsciously to check social media, they would open this app instead, which will only show a countdown until the time the user is allowed to check social media. The screen will then turn dark after showing the countdown. By repeatedly using this app whenever the user picks up the phone to check social media, it is designed to break the habit of opening the social media apps subconsciously.

The app received a couple thousand downloads from around the world, which shows that many people around the world struggle from social media addiction.
